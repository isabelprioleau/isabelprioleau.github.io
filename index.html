Welcome!

Hi, I'm Isabel. I'm a Ph.D. student and Carl J. Friedrich Fellow in the Department of Government at Harvard University, where I study the comparative politics of democracy in Western Europe and North America.

You can find my CV here.

My research focuses broadly on the intersection of social policy, party strategies, and political geography. I am particularly interested in how redistribution interacts with spatial inequalities, how far-right vs. mainstream parties build social policy programs, and the political economy of "left behind" places.

Prior to Harvard, I received a B.A. in Political Science and a certificate in French (summa cum laude) from Yale University in 2025.
